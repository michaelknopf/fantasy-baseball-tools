Latest fantasy baseball bullpen updates

  • Recent usage suggests that Andrez Munoz will not exclusively be used as the closer. Ryne Stanek is most likely to garner the save chances when Munoz is used earlier in the game. (April 21)

  • The Angels announced on April 16 that Robert Stephenson will miss the 2023 season with an elbow injury, solidifying Carlos Estevez 's status as the team's closer. (April 17)

  • Abner Uribe pitched the eighth inning with Joel Payamps handling the ninth-inning save chance (a successful one) on April 16, meaning these two are probably now be co-closers. (April 17)

  • The Blue Jays activated both Jordan Romano and Erik Swanson from the injured list on April 16. Romano should resume his duties as closer, with Swanson serving in a setup capacity. (April 16)

  • Kirby Yates has now worked the ninth inning, with David Robertson handling the eighth, of each of the Rangers' April 12 and 15 wins. It appears Yates is their preferred closer for now. (April 16)

KEY: "Closer" is either that team's officially designated closer, or the pitcher most likely to get the team's next save chance. Teams can be listed with "Co-closers" or "Closer-by-committee." "Primary setup" is the pitcher next-most likely to take over if something happens to the closer, the top holds-getter and the one who might get any save chances on the closer's nights off. "Secondary setup" is the pitcher third-in-line to close and a good candidate for holds. "Middle relief" covers any other pertinent relievers from that team's bullpen. "Sleeper" is another reliever who could provide a decent number of holds or eventually graduate into the closer role.
